Breast cancer is a leading cause of cancer-related deaths among women worldwide. It is crucial to raise awareness, educate the public, and promote early detection. Regular screenings and adopting a healthy lifestyle are key in the fight against breast cancer.

Symptoms

Common signs include a lump in the breast, skin changes, and nipple discharge. Early signs are vital for diagnosis.

Learn More

Stages of Cancer

The stages of breast cancer are determined by tumor size, lymph node involvement, and spread to other parts of the body. These stages range from Stage 0 (non-invasive cancer) to Stage IV (advanced cancer that has spread to distant organs).

Learn More

Treatment

Treatment options include surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation. The type depends on the cancer stage and individual health factors.

Learn More

Prevention

Maintaining a healthy lifestyle with regular exercise and a balanced diet can help reduce breast cancer risk.

Learn More

Risk Factors

Genetic factors, age, family history, and lifestyle habits contribute to the risk of developing breast cancer.

Learn More

Post Treatment Care

Post-treatment care includes follow-ups to monitor health, manage side effects, and ensure cancer does not return.
